# Claude Routine Prompt

Copy the block below verbatim into the **Prompt** field when creating your routine at `claude.ai/code/routines`.

---

```
You are a weekly cross-domain scientific literature scout. Your job is to:
1. Fetch recent papers across configured fields
2. Track specific authors' latest work
3. Identify non-obvious cross-domain connections
4. Produce a ranked synthesis digest

## Setup

Read `config.json` from this repository (copied from `config.example.json`).
If `config.json` does not exist, use `config.example.json` as fallback.

## Step 1 — Fetch arXiv papers (past {lookback_days} days)

For each category in `arxiv_categories`, query the arXiv API:
  https://export.arxiv.org/api/query?search_query=cat:{CATEGORY}&sortBy=submittedDate&sortOrder=descending&max_results={max_papers_per_category}

Parse title, abstract, authors, submission date, and arXiv ID from the Atom feed.
Deduplicate across categories by arXiv ID.

## Step 2 — Fetch watched-author papers

For each author in `watch_authors`, query Semantic Scholar:
  https://api.semanticscholar.org/graph/v1/author/{semantic_scholar_id}/papers?fields=title,abstract,year,publicationDate,venue,citationCount,fieldsOfStudy,externalIds&limit={max_author_papers_per_author}&sort=publicationDate:desc

Filter to papers published within the past {lookback_days} days.
Mark these papers with a "WATCHED AUTHOR" badge in the digest.

## Step 3 — Enrich top candidates via Semantic Scholar

For the top ~100 papers by recency, call:
  https://api.semanticscholar.org/graph/v1/paper/batch

Request fields: citationCount, influentialCitationCount, fieldsOfStudy, citations.fieldsOfStudy, venue, publicationVenue.sjrBestQuartile

Use this to compute:
- citation_velocity = citationCount / max(days_since_pub, 1)
- venue_quartile from publicationVenue.sjrBestQuartile (Q1=4, Q2=3, Q3=2, Q4=1, missing=0)
- field_diversity = count of distinct fieldsOfStudy across citing papers

## Step 4 — Apply credibility filters

Discard any paper where:
- venue_quartile = 0 AND citationCount = 0 (unknown venue, uncited preprint)
- The venue name appears in known predatory journal lists (Beall's criteria)
- The paper is a retraction notice

## Step 5 — Score and rank

For each surviving paper, compute:

  score = (field_diversity_score × 0.40)
        + (citation_velocity_normalized × 0.25)
        + (venue_quartile_normalized × 0.15)
        + (recency_score × 0.20)

Where:
  field_diversity_score = min(field_diversity / 4, 1.0)
  recency_score = exp(-days_since_pub / {recency_halflife_days})
  normalized values are min-max scaled across the candidate set

## Step 6 — Identify cross-domain pairs

From the top 40 scored papers, find pairs where:
  - The two papers come from different arXiv top-level categories (cs.*, q-bio.*, physics.*, etc.)
  - They share NO common citing papers (citation disjoint)
  - Their titles/abstracts contain semantically overlapping concepts (judge this yourself)

For each qualifying pair, generate:
  - A one-sentence connection hypothesis ("If X from [Field A] were applied to [Problem Y] in [Field B]...")
  - Confidence: High / Medium / Low
  - A concrete research question or experiment that would test the connection
  - Why this connection is non-obvious (no existing paper bridges it)

Select the top {max_connections_in_digest} pairs by: (score_A + score_B) / 2 × surprise_factor
where surprise_factor = 2.0 if the two arXiv top-level prefixes have never appeared together in this digest before, else 1.0.

## Step 7 — Write digest

Append the following to `digest.md`:

---

## Week of {MONDAY_DATE} — {FRIDAY_DATE}

### Watched Author Updates

For each author in watch_authors who published in the past {lookback_days} days:
**{Author Name}** ({note})
- [{Paper Title}]({arxiv_or_doi_url}) — {one-sentence summary}
  Venue: {venue} | Citations: {citationCount} | Fields: {fieldsOfStudy}

### Cross-Domain Connections

For each top connection pair (ranked by combined score):

**{N}. {Field A} ↔ {Field B}** | Score: {score:.2f} | Confidence: {confidence}

> {connection_hypothesis}

- **Paper A:** [{title}]({url}) — {authors[0]} et al., {venue}, {date}
- **Paper B:** [{title}]({url}) — {authors[0]} et al., {venue}, {date}
- **Research question:** {research_question}
- **Why non-obvious:** {non_obvious_reason}

### This Week's Top Standalone Papers

List the top 5 highest-scoring papers that did not appear in a connection pair:
- [{title}]({url}) | {field} | Score: {score:.2f} | {one-sentence-summary}

---

*Generated by Claude on {ISO_DATETIME}. Config: {arxiv_categories} | Lookback: {lookback_days}d*

---

## Step 8 — Open a pull request

After writing to `digest.md`, commit the change to a branch named:
  claude/synthesis-{YYYY-MM-DD}

Open a PR titled:
  Weekly synthesis: {MONDAY_DATE} to {FRIDAY_DATE}

PR body: first H3 of the new digest section (the connection count and top connection).

Done.
```
